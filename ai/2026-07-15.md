## Counter-narrative

The day's biggest story is China's Anthropomorphic AI framework going live. The skeptical case:

- **China moving first on AI-agent regulation is less strategically important than the coverage implies.** The framework binds Chinese domestic providers and licensed foreign JV structures — most non-Chinese enterprises never exposed consumer agent/persona features to Chinese users at scale in the first place, because market access constraints already made that unattractive. What actually changes is the shape of the *Chinese* consumer agent market: it becomes filed-and-licensed, minor-restricted and traceability-instrumented. That is real, but it does not automatically become a global compliance template, and the frequent framing of "China ahead of the EU on agent regulation" flatters both regulators; the EU AI Act's high-risk framework covers a much wider surface (employment, education, credit, essential services) than the CAC framework does. Enterprises should treat the CAC rules as important-if-you-have-China-exposure and *modestly* informative for EU DPIA work, not as a global compliance signal.
